Section 1: Listening

40 questions • Approximately 30 minutes • You will hear each recording only once

The Listening test consists of 4 parts. You will hear a variety of recordings and answer questions based on what you hear. The recordings include conversations and monologues in various contexts.

Part 1: Conversation between two people in an everyday social context

Part 2: Monologue in an everyday social context

Part 3: Conversation between up to four people in an educational or training context

Part 4: Monologue on an academic subject

Section 2: Reading

40 questions • 60 minutes • Read passages and answer questions

The Reading test consists of 3 passages with a variety of question types. You will read texts taken from books, journals, magazines and newspapers.

Passage 1

A small coffee shop called "Morning Bean" opened last month in Minh's neighbourhood. The owner, Lan, sells Vietnamese drip coffee and fresh sandwiches. The shop opens at 6 a.m. on weekdays because many office workers stop for breakfast. Prices are lower before 8 a.m.; customers get a 20 percent discount if they bring their own cup. At lunchtime the place is full, so Lan plans to add outdoor tables next week. She also wants to create an online menu so people can order ahead and pick up their food quickly.

Passage 2

Last year, a small town planted 500 young trees along its main streets to reduce air pollution. Volunteers watered the trees twice a week during the dry season. After six months, nearly 90 percent of the trees were still healthy. The town council measured air quality and found that dust levels dropped by five percent compared to the previous year. Because of this success, the council will plant 300 more trees next spring and set up wooden benches under the largest ones. Local schools will use the project to teach students about climate change and environmental protection.

Passage 3

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues of our time, affecting every aspect of human life and the natural world. Scientists have been studying the effects of global warming for decades, and their findings consistently show that human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, are the primary cause of recent climate change. The consequences are far-reaching, including rising sea levels, more frequent extreme weather events, and threats to biodiversity. Governments and organizations worldwide are implementing various strategies to mitigate these effects and adapt to the changing climate.

Section 4: Speaking

3 parts • 11-14 minutes • Face-to-face interview with an examiner

The Speaking test is a face-to-face interview with an examiner. It consists of 3 parts: Part 1 (Introduction and interview), Part 2 (Individual long turn), and Part 3 (Two-way discussion).

Part 1: Introduction and Interview (4-5 minutes)

The examiner will ask you general questions about yourself and familiar topics such as home, family, work, studies and interests.

Sample Questions:

  • What is your full name?
  • Where are you from?
  • Do you work or study?
  • What do you like about your job/studies?
  • What do you do in your free time?
0 words

Part 2: Individual Long Turn (3-4 minutes)

You will be given a task card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare and then speak for 1-2 minutes on the topic.

Task Card:

Describe a memorable trip you have taken.

You should say:

  • where you went
  • when you went there
  • who you went with
  • and explain why this trip was memorable for you

Part 3: Two-way Discussion (4-5 minutes)

The examiner will ask you further questions connected to the topic in Part 2. These questions are more abstract and require you to discuss, analyze and speculate.

Sample Questions:

  • How has tourism changed in your country over the past decade?
  • What are the benefits and drawbacks of traveling alone?
  • Do you think technology has made travel easier or more complicated?
